# SAFRA Vendor Engagement SOP

## Goal
Create a faster delivery loop between EVVO and the vendor by keeping EVVO as the internal shaping layer and sending the vendor only the stabilized, optimized package.

## Observed collaboration pattern
- Customer ideas arrive first.
- EVVO incorporates the initial development into the system.
- EVVO and the vendor align internally before the vendor engages the customer.
- The vendor then optimizes the package for delivery.
- PM visibility is broadened by adding the right people to the thread early.

## SOP
1. Intake
- Capture the customer idea or change request in the EVVO thread.
- Convert it into a clear internal note with scope, assumptions, and desired outcome.

2. Internal shaping
- EVVO translates the idea into an initial build or system update.
- Keep the first pass lightweight and concrete.
- Standardize the format where possible so the vendor receives a consistent artifact.

3. EVVO-vendor kickoff
- Hold an internal kickoff between EVVO and the vendor before any customer-facing handoff.
- Confirm ownership, scope, timing, and dependencies.
- Resolve open questions inside the EVVO-vendor lane, not with the customer.

4. Vendor optimization
- Send the vendor the stabilized internal package.
- Vendor refines, optimizes, and prepares the delivery-ready version.
- Use the vendor for acceleration, not for first-pass interpretation.

5. Customer engagement
- Only engage the customer after EVVO and vendor are aligned.
- Present a single, coherent direction.
- Avoid letting the customer become the intermediary between EVVO and the vendor.

6. Thread hygiene
- Add relevant PMs early so the thread has the right visibility.
- Keep one source of truth in the thread for scope and decisions.
- Use short acknowledgements for confirmations, but preserve the decision trail in the main thread.

## Operating rules
- EVVO owns internal synthesis.
- Vendor owns optimization and polish.
- Customer gets the final aligned result, not the raw internal debate.
- Speed comes from reducing back-and-forth, not skipping alignment.

## Suggested name for the methodology
- EVVO Gate-and-Optimize Loop
- EVVO Internal Shape, Vendor Optimize, Customer Receive
- Align first, optimize second, engage customer last

## Why this works
- Prevents the vendor from re-interpreting incomplete ideas.
- Reduces customer-facing churn.
- Makes internal handoff cleaner.
- Increases delivery speed by front-loading alignment.
